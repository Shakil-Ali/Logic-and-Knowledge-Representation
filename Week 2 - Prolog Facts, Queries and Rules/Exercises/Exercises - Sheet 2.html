INST0072: Exercises for Lecture 2

Exercise 2-1

Add the following two pieces of information to the program 'foodPreferences.pl' [ view | download ] from Lecture 2:

  1. Rob likes food that both John and Mary like.
  2. Antonis likes food that either Sue likes or Chris likes.

Your solution should result in the following input/output when tested with SWI Prolog: 

    ?- likes(rob, X).
    X = big_mac ;
    false.

    ?- likes(antonis, X).
    X = apple_pie ;
    X = ice_cream ;
    X = milkshake.

Exercise 2-2

The diagram below gives a graphical representation of the 'parent' relation implied by the program 'mothersAndFathers.pl' [ view | download ] from Lecture 2.

(click here to view diagram in larger PDF format)

Add definitions for the predicates 'parent/2', 'grandmother/2', 'son/2', 'daughter/2', 'grandson/2', 'granddaughter/2', 'brother/2', 'sister/2', 'aunt/2', 'uncle/2', 'niece/2' and 'nephew/2' to mothersAndFathers.pl. (If it helps, change the set of facts for 'father/2', 'mother/2', 'male/1' and 'female/1' to reflect your own family.) Test the program thoroughly. (Do not worry about "repeat answers" when you enter ';'.)

HINTS:

  1. To specify that the variables X and Y are different, write:

        X \= Y
  2. You may find it convenient to define a predicate 'partner/2' before defining 'aunt/2', 'uncle/2', 'niece/2' and 'nephew/2' (where two people are partners if they share a son or daughter).
